Events

Come see the Studio in the open.

A lot of what a venture studio does is invisible from the outside — gate calls, kills, the slow work of validation. These are the places we make it visible: a recurring Showcase, a December signature moment, free monthly sessions, and the conferences where we go looking for the next idea and the next operator.

The signature moment

December: the Solutions Festival.

The Studio's year builds toward a December signature moment: a Solutions Festival, paired with a Hackathon and a Family Flourishing Challenge, where the field, our partners, and the people building inside the Studio gather around the work in one place.

It's the moment the portfolio meets the field — less a pitch day than a working session on the problems worth solving for children, families, and the workforce around them. We'll publish the format and how to take part closer to the date.

The calendar

A standing rhythm, so showing up becomes a habit.

From June 2026

Studio Showcase

A 60-minute hybrid look inside the portfolio. We walk the map of what's in motion, then tell two founder stories — one advancing and one exited — with equal airtime, the same format, and the same dignity. Featuring the exits is the point; a showcase that only celebrates winners would reproduce exactly the dynamic we're trying to undo. Founder stories run only with the founder's consent, and come down on request, no questions asked. Kicks off June 2026, recurring.

Monthly

Free webinars & education sessions

Value-first sessions for anyone working at the intersection of children's mental health and the workforce that supports it. No sales pitch — the same mechanic that compounded an audience around the IECMHC certificate work.

Monthly

Office hours

A standing 30-minute window for prospective idea-bringers to talk a problem through before submitting anything. No commitment, no polished pitch. There's no "ready" you have to be — that's what the window is for.

Throughout the year

Where we'll be

We source ideas and operators at the field's own gatherings rather than renting our own stage — ZERO TO THREE, NAEYC, and the AAP National Conference among them. If you'll be there, come find us and bring the problem keeping you up at night.
