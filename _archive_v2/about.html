About

A venture arm for thirty years of work.

Thrive Studio is the venture studio inside Georgetown's Thrive Center for Children, Families, and Communities. We build ventures at the intersection of children's mental health and the workforce that supports it — the educators, home visitors, and caregivers in the room with the child.

Why we exist

The grants model is closing. The work needs surfaces that pay for themselves.

For most of its thirty years the Center ran under a different name, built around federal grants, IECMH consultation programs, and philanthropy. That model didn't fail — it produced a body of applied research and trainings that genuinely shifted how states deliver early-childhood mental-health work. But the conditions that made it work are eroding. Federal technical-assistance funding is being cut. State budgets are constrained. Foundation cycles are shorter. Programs that served communities for years end mid-cohort because a grant didn't renew.

A thirty-year accumulation of knowledge can't keep being delivered through a mechanism that requires re-justifying its existence every two years. So we built a different vehicle alongside the research, training, and partnership work — not instead of it. The Studio takes what the Center knows and turns it into surfaces of intervention that pay for themselves: products, platforms, and services that generate enough revenue to keep going regardless of who's writing checks that quarter.

That's the whole reason the Studio exists. Not to chase exits — to make a thirty-year body of work durable.

Leadership

A deliberately tight gate. Three reads, three centers of gravity.

Dr. Matt Biel
Director, Thrive Center

Child psychiatrist; holds the Marriott Endowed Chair and directs the Center. He carries the institutional authority — when the question is whether a venture aligns with the mission or touches a Center-level commitment, his read is load-bearing.

Jason Lehmbeck
Studio Lead

The deepest venture-formation and financial-modeling experience on the team. Equity structures, return scenarios, and spin-out criteria are his territory — when the question is whether the venture math actually works, his read is load-bearing.

Jean-Baptiste Passot
Technology & Innovation

Runs the operating cadence, owns prototype build for most ventures, and is the Studio's outward technical face — when the question is whether a venture is tractable and the prototype is producing real signal, his read is load-bearing.

Three people make a real go/no-go at every gate. The membership is tight on purpose: three people produce real calls, larger groups litigate. A stage gate only works if a real no is possible, and a small body is what keeps that real.

How decisions widen

The committee decides. Wider rings inform, then it's announced.

  • Gate Committee — Matt, Jason, and JB. Every stage-gate call and every kill sits here, and nowhere else.
  • Leadership ring — adds Dr. Neal Horen and Shai Idelson for clinical and go-to-market input before a close call. They feed the read; they don't vote the gate.
  • Operating ring — adds Mariam Kherbouch, Sunny Patel, and rotating workstream leads for cross-venture coordination, resource allocation, and partnership routing.
  • Announced broadly — every decision is surfaced past the committee at the next leadership meeting and Sprint Demo, so the team knows what changed and why. Announcing isn't deciding.
Where we sit

Inside Georgetown — and clear about the lines.

01

Part of the Thrive Center

The Center is a Georgetown unit; the Studio is one of its programs, reporting up to Matt and using Center brand, infrastructure, and IP under Georgetown's umbrella. Georgetown's Office of Technology Commercialization handles IP and spin-out mechanics for any venture that leaves.

02

A Center of Excellence

The Thrive Center is the SAMHSA-designated National Center of Excellence for IECMHC, with roughly thirty years of applied research and licensable IP behind it. Research authority, practitioner trust, and regulatory standing in one place.

03

Not the Innovation Hub

The Thrive Innovation Hub is a separate Center program — a fellowship for already-formed external startups. Similar branding, different operation. We generate and build ideas in-house; the Hub backs founders who arrive with their own. Worth not conflating.
