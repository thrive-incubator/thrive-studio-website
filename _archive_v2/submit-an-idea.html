Bring us the problem keeping you up at night.

If you're a clinician or researcher who sees a problem in the field — for children, families, and communities — this is the front door. You don't have to be a Thrive employee, and you don't have to be the one who builds it. There's no "ready" you have to be first.

Idea-bringer ≠ operator

Two roles, two doors. This is the idea door.

Most ideas here come from clinicians and researchers who see a problem cold — not from operators arriving with a company already formed. You can bring the problem without committing to run the venture. When an idea is validated, the Studio finds and pairs the right operator; final team composition is decided by fit with the work, not by who submitted it.

You don't have to be inside the Thrive Center, and you don't have to know the business model yet. We can find the hypothesis with you. The one thing we ask is that you've watched the problem closely enough to be sure it's real.

If you're an experienced operator looking to lead a venture instead, that's the other door — For Operators →

What fits

A few criteria, so the right ideas find us.

01

Works through the system

Strengthens the workforce already in the room — educators, home visitors, child care providers, and the caregivers around the family — rather than routing around it.

02

B2B or B2G

The buyer is an institution — a program, a district, a state, a health system — not an individual parent. The reach is the workforce, not the consumer.

03

Prevention-shaped

Builds capacity before crises emerge, where the cost-to-impact ratio is highest — not symptom management after the fact.

04

Sustainable, not venture-scale

Could become a profitable $3–15M ARR business that pays its own way — not a moonshot, and not a grant looking for a home.

What happens after you submit

A response at every step. Always in writing.

01

Acknowledged within two weeks

Every submission gets a written acknowledgment. Nothing disappears into a void — you'll know we have it.

Your timeUnder 30 minutes, once
02

Monthly screen against public criteria

We review against mission fit, the gap in the field, scope feasibility, and current capacity. The criteria are public and don't change inside a cycle. You get written feedback either way.

OutcomeAdvanced · held · or out of current scope
03

Held ideas don't expire

An idea that doesn't advance now isn't gone — it waits in the idea bank, gets revisited each quarter, and is surfaced at the Showcase or referred to partners when a fit emerges. The same idea can be "out of scope" one quarter and "advance" the next, because the call was always about fit and timing, not idea quality.

House languageAdvanced · held · exited — never "picked" or "rejected"

Want to think it through first?

Office hours are a standing monthly 30-minute window to talk an idea through before you submit. No commitment, no polished pitch required — there is no "ready" you have to be. Most people leave with a sharper one-sentence version of the problem than they walked in with.
