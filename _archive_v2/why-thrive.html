Why not VC

Same words. Different bet.

We share a lot of vocabulary with venture capital — pipeline, validation, equity, spin-out, MVP — and we differ on almost everything that matters. This page is the filter. If the table below reads as a problem rather than the point, we're probably not for you, and that's fine.

Line by line

The same eight words. Two different bets.

The simplest framing

VC bets on outsized exits. We bet on a portfolio that compounds mission.

VC backs founders who already know what they want to build, and bets on outsized exits. The Studio identifies what should be built from inside an institution that already knows the problem cold, finds the right operator for it, and bets on a portfolio of mid-sized companies that compound mission impact while paying their own way. The whole pipeline has to work — there's no single moonshot carrying the rest.

In shape, we're closer to venture studio models in adjacent fields — Idealab, Atomic, Pioneer Square Labs — than to a fund. What's unusual is the mission-aligned governance and the institutional anchor inside Georgetown. Most studios optimize for founder upside; we optimize for the durability of the work the Center has spent thirty years on.

What this predicts

A few choices follow directly. They're not arbitrary.

  • We avoid B2C / direct-to-parent ventures — even when the unit economics look attractive. The leverage is the workforce around the child, not the consumer.
  • We won't chase unicorn-shaped opportunities — even when one walks past. Wrong portfolio math, wrong operator profile, wrong relationship with the Center after spin-out.
  • We invest heavily upstream in validation — desk research, customer discovery, MVP — because the Studio takes operational risk where VC takes capital risk. Our bet has to be made before there's a company.
  • Spin-outs retain a Thrive stake and an ongoing relationship. Closer to a coalition than a clean break. That's not a hedge — it's how the model funds itself.
  • We say no a lot. Two spin-outs a year against 20–25 ideas vetted means most get killed. The four-stage gate exists to be honest about which ones shouldn't ship.

If that's the bet you want to make, let's talk.
