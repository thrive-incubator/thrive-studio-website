The thesis

More clinicians won't close the gap. The leverage is the adults already in the room.

There will never be enough clinicians, and the pipeline can't be scaled fast enough to close the gap in a generation. The children who need the most help spend almost none of their hours with a clinician — they spend them with early-childhood educators, home visitors, and caregivers. Equip that workforce and you reach an order of magnitude more children than any clinical model.

The evidence base has a name: Infant & Early Childhood Mental Health Consultation — "a preventative, capacity-building intervention in which mental health professionals partner with early childhood professionals to indirectly improve the environments and relationships that young children experience." (Silver et al., 2022)

What makes us different

Six things that are true here — and almost nowhere else.

01

Workforce-enabling thesis

We build companies that equip the adults around the child — not more clinicians, not B2C apps. No other studio we know of owns this framing.

02

A credibility moat

Anchored in a SAMHSA-designated National Center of Excellence with ~30 years of applied research, practitioner trust, and licensable IP.

03

Ideas sourced inside

Brought by clinicians and researchers and developed in-house — not external founders pitching their own startups. Rare among studios, unique in this domain.

04

Non-unicorn economics

$3–15M ARR, profitable, 3–5x, ~80% survival target, capital recycling. A deliberate counter-position to VC orthodoxy. No unicorn required.

05

Three honest outcomes

Spin out, spin in, or shut down — with the post-mortems published. Radical transparency that institutional buyers and clinician idea-sources can trust.

06

B2B / B2G, prevention-shaped

Buyers are school districts, states, and health systems — where reimbursement and policy momentum actually are. We build through those systems, not around them.

Proof it's real

Attunify.

Our first independent spin-out, launched after a roughly five-month incubation. The founding cohort opened in June 2026 — defining the national competency standard for Infant & Early Childhood Mental Health Consultation, the missing tier between teacher training and clinical care. Not theoretical; already enrolling and generating revenue.
