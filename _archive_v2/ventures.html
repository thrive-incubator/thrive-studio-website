First spin-out · live

Attunify. Live

Our first independent spin-out, launched after a roughly five-month incubation. The founding cohort opened in June 2026. Attunify is defining the national competency standard for Infant & Early Childhood Mental Health Consultation (IECMHC) — the missing tier between teacher training (Tier 1) and clinical care (Tier 3) — and building the certification to scale a workforce of qualified consultants.

It runs a dual-product model. The IECMH Consultant Certification launched June 2026 — a four-month foundational program followed by eight months of live supervision, founding cohort priced at $2,500. The Educator Support Platform, a B2B SaaS for the broader early-childhood workforce, launches in 2027. Certify the senior tier first, then push that body of practice down into the daily hands of the much larger educator population.

The kills

We show the post-mortems on purpose.

A killed venture is the gate doing its job — saving the team and the field months of misdirected effort. We say no a lot; that's the point. Several ideas sit paused or in the idea bank, and one was formally shut down in the past year.

Thrive Compass

Shut down · Standalone CHW platform

A standalone community-health-worker platform. High internal enthusiasm met thin, contradictory buyer signal — so we stopped, with a clean post-mortem. The workforce thesis it was testing lives on elsewhere in the pipeline.

The Showcase

A recurring, hybrid look inside the portfolio.

The Studio Showcase is the mechanism that makes the process visible and predictable. Each session walks the portfolio map, tells one advancing founder's story and one exited founder's story — equal airtime, same format, same dignity — and names what we're sourcing next. It kicks off in June 2026.

It's not a pitch event, not a status report, not a celebration of winners. Featuring exits with the same dignity as advances is what makes it work. Founder stories are published only with the founder's explicit consent, and come down — no questions asked — if a founder ever asks.
